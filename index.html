Who We Are

A Nigerian answer to a Nigerian problem.

Our mission is simple: to make Nigeria the place where Nigerian aircraft are maintained.

About AFMS Meridian

Closing aviation's most expensive gap.

We exist to reverse one of the most persistent drains on Nigerian aviation: the absence of accessible, world-class heavy maintenance at home.

AFMS Meridian Limited was formed to close one of the most expensive gaps in Nigerian aviation — the absence of a fully equipped facility capable of comprehensive heavy maintenance.

For years, Nigerian operators have been compelled to ferry aircraft to Ethiopia, Morocco, Egypt, the UAE, Italy and South Africa for major checks — paying in scarce foreign exchange, losing weeks of revenue per aircraft, and exporting the engineering jobs that should belong to Nigerians.

We are being built as a full-spectrum MRO provider serving commercial airlines, business aviation, charter operators and government fleets across Nigeria and the West African region.

Mission, Vision & Values

  • MISSION Safe, reliable, internationally certified maintenance in Nigeria — eliminating capital flight and aircraft-on-ground time.
  • VISION The leading independent MRO hub for Northern Nigeria and the West African corridor.
  • VALUES Safety above all · engineering integrity · indigenous capability · operational reliability · transparency.
The Founders' Insight

Built by people who saw the problem from the inside.

AFMS Meridian was founded by a group of experienced civilian and military aviation professionals who saw the same problem from the inside — and decided to build the institution Nigeria has needed for two decades.

They saw Nigerian Air Force engineering depth, a talent pipeline trained at NCAT Zaria, and a domestic fleet starved of timely maintenance. AFMS Meridian brings those assets together under one roof, in one underserved geography, with one purpose: airworthiness, delivered at home.

AFMS Meridian Limited is in active development. References to facilities and certifications describe our planned build-out and regulatory roadmap.

Regional Hub
Why Dutse · Why The North

The right facility in the right place.

Nigeria's existing and announced MRO capacity clusters in Lagos, Abuja, Kaduna and Uyo. The North-West and North-East — and the regional traffic they anchor — have no dedicated commercial MRO. That is the gap we fill.

GEOGRAPHY

A true Northern hub

Dutse serves Kano, Kaduna, Abuja and regional West African traffic without the congestion and slot pressure of the southern airports.

TALENT

Proximity to capability

Close to Nigerian Air Force engineering infrastructure and the trained talent pool of NCAT Zaria — the country's principal aviation training institution.

WHITE SPACE

An uncontested geography

Rather than competing head-on with southern facilities, AFMS Meridian serves an underserved region — and the airlines that operate it daily.

POLICY

Aligned with national priorities

The Federal Government has publicly committed to ending maintenance capital flight. A Northern MRO advances airport utilisation, regional jobs and that national goal.

No domestic facility currently performs comprehensive C-checks — so the work, and the money, leaves the country.

Domestic carriers are compelled to ferry aircraft to Ethiopia, Italy, Morocco, Egypt, the UAE and South Africa for heavy maintenance. Estimates of the resulting annual capital flight range from official figures of around $200 million to industry estimates exceeding $1 billion every year.

With a single C-check costing $4–6 million and falling due every 12 to 18 months, the economics of a credible domestic MRO are compelling — and the Federal Government has signalled clear intent to keep that spend at home.

Safety & Compliance

Airworthiness is not negotiable.

Safety and regulatory integrity are the foundation of everything we build — for our clients, their passengers, and the regulator.

NCAA AMO Pathway

AFMS Meridian is building toward certification as a Nigerian Civil Aviation Authority Approved Maintenance Organisation (AMO) — the regulatory foundation for all scheduled work.

International Alignment

Our procedures are designed to align with EASA Part-145 and FAA equivalents, supporting confidence among operators and lessors.

Safety Management System

An independent SMS embeds hazard identification, risk management and a just safety culture across every function.

Independent Quality Assurance

Our Quality function reports outside the production line — ensuring that airworthiness, not throughput, always has the final word.
